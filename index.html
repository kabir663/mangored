Mango Tree Information

Overview

The mango tree (Mangifera indica) is a tropical evergreen tree widely celebrated as the "king of fruits". Originating in South Asia, this tree can grow up to 30–40 meters tall and live for over 100 years, continuously producing fruits throughout its lifetime. Its leathery, dark green leaves and fragrant, pale yellow flowers are key characteristics. Mangoes are not only delicious but also nutritionally rich, making them highly valued worldwide.

Interesting Facts

  • Mango is the national fruit of India.
  • Mango trees have existed for over 25 million years, as suggested by fossil evidence.
  • The tree’s flowers are male and hermaphroditic, aiding efficient pollination.

Benefits

  1. Health: Boosts immunity, aids digestion, and improves skin health.
  2. Environmental: Acts as an air purifier and prevents soil erosion.
  3. Economic: Fruits provide income; wood is used in construction.
  4. Cultural: Symbolizes prosperity; leaves are used in rituals.

Leaves, Flowers, and Fruits

Leaves: Mango leaves are lance-shaped and leathery, growing spirally on branches. Young leaves appear reddish before maturing into dark green. They are used medicinally for treating diabetes and burns.

Flowers: The tree produces pale yellow clusters called panicles, containing hundreds of flowers. These are both male and hermaphroditic.

Fruits: Mangoes are oval-shaped, weighing 150g–2kg, with skin colors ranging from green to red. Varieties include Alphonso, Kesar, and Totapuri.
